Our Mission

Keeping the public informed on their current local air quality

As of September 20 2020, there are currently 79 large bushfires in the United States, burning over 3 million hectares of land. Five of the ten largest recorded bushfires have raged during this year's fire season, with the incidence of fires projected to further increase as the global climate warms, according to the US Government's Global Change Research Program. These burning bushfires produce fine particulate matter that can be hazardous to the lungs. An air quality index (AQI) is used to communicate the level of air polution to the public, with the health risk increasing as air quality index increases.

Wildfire Watch is capable of sending you SMS alerts when your local AQI breaches a hazardous level threshold. Additionally, you can search for the local AQI for a desired zip code at any time.
